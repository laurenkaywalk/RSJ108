How to relax as a journalist ...

Time: 5 minutes to numerous hours
Helpfullness rating: 100%

Are you wondering how to wind down and relax after a busy day of being a journalist? Below you can find 5 tips for relaxing like a journalist!

  • Things You'll Need

  • Helathy Snacks: Mango, Dark Chocolate, Gum, Complex Carbs or Somethign Crunchy
  • Music Source: Iphone, Ipod, Radio, etc.
  • Book or Journal
  • Essential Oils & (optional) Diffuser
  • Tea Bag, Cup & (optional)Suagr, Honey & Milk

  1. Snack (healthy!) to destress.

    When it comes to food, they can work for or against your body and your mind. It turns out that certain foods cue our brains to give off certain hormones and put us in our happy places. Here’s a few you can try: Mango to lower cortisol levels (that little bugger of a hormone that makes us feel stressed). Dark chocolate a little over an ounce can reportedly calm the nerves and even start to stabilize metabolism levels.Chewing gum (the act of repeated chewing, actually) can help fight anxiety and stress, combating pretty much any negative mood.Complex carbs, like whole-grain breads and whole oatmeal can prompt the brain to produce serotonin.Something crunchy, nuts cerelry or pretzels.It is reported that stressed individuals often crave crunchy foods, and that’s likely linked back to chewing (that fights anxiety, as listed above).

    Example of a dark chocolate bar.

  2. Put on your favorite song.

    Studies show that listening to calming music that you like can help lower your blood pressure, heart rate, and reduce perceived levels of anxiety.Not just claming music, it is whatever YOU find calming! It doesn’t hurt to dance it out, either. Not only is dance a great way to get in your cardio workout, but there’s science to back up the fact that it can lower anxiety, too. So hop on your bed and start getting jiggy for the sake of your body and your mind.

  3. Read a good book or write in your journal.

    Research says that writing down your worries gets rid of them and helps you feel more confident, which can improve outcomes. Or try hankering down with a good book. If it’s full of jokes, even better. If you’re looking for something a little more constructive, try a word or crossword puzzle to get your mind away from the stress and into the problem-solving zone.

    Example of reading a book.

  4. Try aromatherapy.

    The soothing scents of aromatherapy travel up our olfactory system and into our brain, taking over where stress and anxiety once set up camp. Just a whiff of one of these smells can bring a relaxing feeling over your entire body. Rose, bergamot, lavender, orange, lemon, and sandalwood are all great options. But whatever scent makes you feel good is good, too.

  5. Grab a cup of tea.

    Did you know that chamomile, passionflower, and green teas all have stress-fighting effects? Yep. They’ve been found to reduce anger and even fight depressionIf you’re not a fan of these teas plain, try them with a teaspoon of honey for a total win-win as honey is proven to be an anxiety-fighter and a mood-uplifter, too.
